## 2. LOGO SYSTEM

### 2.1 Primary Logo

The Alchemetryx mark features a 5-bar arc representing growth, clarity, and transformation. It sits above a clean wordmark.

### 2.2 Logo Variants

- Full logo (arc + wordmark)

- Icon (circular "a" with gold accent)

### 2.3 Clear Space

Maintain padding equal to the height of the shortest bar.

### 2.4 Minimum Size

- Full logo: 160px width (digital)

- Icon: 32px (digital)

### 2.5 Misuse Rules

- No recoloring bars

- No gradients, shadows, or glow

- No distortion/stretch

- Do not place on conflict-heavy backgrounds

## 4. TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

### 4.1 Hierarchy Overview

**Headings (H1–H3): Urbanist**

- Bold / Semi-Bold

- Digital, modern, geometric

**Subheadings (H4–H6): DM Sans**

- Medium / Regular

- For section labels and structured content

**Body Text: DM Sans**

- Regular weight

- Optimized for readability at small sizes

**Brand Accents / Highlights: Spectral**

- Used sparingly

- Italic or Bold for emphasis

### 4.2 Rules

- Limit to 3 weights per page

- Maintain consistent spacing and line-height

- Use Spectral for key words or quotes only

# **ALCHEMETRYX BRAND VOICE (Updated 2025 Edition)**

---

### **8.1 Tone**

### **Clear**

The message must be instantly understood. No complex wording. No unnecessary detail.

### **Direct**

Say what matters. No fluff. No filler. Every sentence should move the reader toward clarity.

### **Intelligent**

Insight-driven, not opinion-driven. Sound like a partner who understands systems, not a vendor who sells services.

### **Calm Confidence**

We never force urgency. We earn trust through precision, logic, and composure.

### **Modern & Global**

The language should feel clean, contemporary, and culturally neutral — suitable for India, the UK, Germany, and global SMEs.

---

# **8.2 Messaging Pillars**

### **Simplicity**

Make complex problems feel solvable.

&#x20;Focus on what matters. Reduce cognitive load.

&#x20;The outcome should always feel “simple, structured, doable.”

### **Intelligence**

Every statement should reflect thoughtful understanding of how SMEs operate.

&#x20;Show clarity, insight, and context — not hype.

### **Structure**

Everything we say should reinforce our core belief:

**Structured systems create scalable outcomes.**

&#x20;This underpins Decision Intelligence, Platforms, and Automation.

### **Growth**

Our voice must show that clarity, structure, and intelligent execution lead to measurable results.

&#x20;Never growth for the sake of growth — growth backed by clarity.

---

# **8.3 Writing Rules**

## **Do**

✓ Use short, clean, precise sentences

&#x20;✓ Lead with outcomes, not features

&#x20;✓ Speak like a strategic partner, not a vendor

&#x20;✓ Highlight clarity, structure, and measurable progress

&#x20;✓ Use examples that feel grounded, not theoretical

&#x20;✓ Maintain composure — be assertive, not loud

&#x20;✓ Use simple English with global readability

&#x20;✓ Keep CTA language actionable but calm (e.g., “Explore your pilot,” “See how it works”)

---

## **Don’t**

✗ Don’t use jargon or corporate clichés

&#x20;✗ Don’t exaggerate — no “transform your business instantly,” “guaranteed results,” etc.

&#x20;✗ Don’t sound like a sales pitch

&#x20;✗ Don’t overload sentences with technical detail

&#x20;✗ Don’t use metaphors that require effort to understand

&#x20;✗ Don’t force emotional language — clarity always wins

&#x20;✗ Don’t write in a way that creates confusion or heaviness

---
